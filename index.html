Beyond LA/LA

This is a mapping project that takes another look at Pacific Standard Time: LA/LA. PST LA/LA explores Latin American and Latino art in Los Angeles. This project takes a closer look at the relationship between the museums and other spaces/neighborhoods in which the exhibits take place, especially looking at more where Hispanic and Latino communities exist in Los Angeles and Southern California. One of the aims of this project is to take a more critical approach to understanding the relationship between accessiblity to musuems and the communities that they aim to represent. Another aim is to feature the web mapping platform CARTO as an easy alternative to making functional web maps with filters.

The first map offers all the exhibit locations with two filters, one to sort by admission type and another to sort by exhibit theme. In addition Los Angeles Times neighorhoods are overlayed as a point of reference.

The second map has every exhibit in Pacific Standard Time: LA/LA mapped out again. For this map we have introduced a data layer that shows the Hispanic and Latino population for Los Angeles County from the 2015 American Community Survey.

Explore the Hispanic and Latino community

The American Community Survey provides access to a wealth of demographic data. It provides a detailed glimpse into the life of Los Angeles. The map displays the total number of individuals who have identified themeselves as Hispanic or Latino. One of the most important observations of this project is to show the discrepency between where museums are located in relation to Hispanic and Latino neighborhoods. With few exceptions museums and other instituions fall outside of these communities.
